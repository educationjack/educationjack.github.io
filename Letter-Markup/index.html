Exotic dance moves

Yes, you are right! As part of my post-doctorate work, I studied exotic tribal dances. My favourite dances are below, with definitions:

Polynesian chicken dance
A little-known but influential dance dating back as far as 300 BC: a whole village would dance in a circle like chickens to encourage their livestock to be “fruitful”.
Icelandic Brownian shuffle
Before Icelanders developed fire as a means of getting warm, they practiced this dance—huddling close together on the floor and shuffling in imperceptibly tiny, rapid movements. One of my fellow students said he thought this inspired modern styles such as twerking.
Arctic robot dance
An instance of historic misinformation—English explorers in the 1960s believed they had discovered a new dance characterized by “robotic” movements among Northern Alaskans and Canadians; later it was discovered they were simply very cold.

For more of my research, see my exotic dance research page.
